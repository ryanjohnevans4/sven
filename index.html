England
Capital: London · Governed by UK Parliament

England has the UK's most developed statutory social value framework. PPN 002 aligns procurement directly with the Government's five missions, with mandatory requirements from October 2025.

Key Legislation
📜
Public Services (Social Value) Act 2012
Requires commissioners to consider social value before procurement
📋
Procurement Act 2023
Replaces EU rules; embeds social value more deeply across all procurement
🏛️
PPN 002 — Social Value Model (March 2025)
Mandatory for all in-scope organisations from 1 October 2025
Contract Thresholds
Services & Supplies£213,477+
Works Contracts£5,336,937+
Minimum SV Weighting10% of award criteria
Key Requirements
Minimum 10% social value weighting in all above-threshold central government contracts
Specific, measurable, time-bound commitments using the PPN 002 Model required from bidders
Standard Reporting Metrics must be used to monitor and report performance
Transition period Feb–Sep 2025; mandatory from 1 October 2025

Scotland
Capital: Edinburgh · Scottish Parliament (Holyrood)

Scotland has the most prescriptive statutory framework in the UK for community benefits. Fair Work First goes significantly further than the rest of the UK on workers' rights.

Key Legislation
🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿
Procurement Reform (Scotland) Act 2014
Statutory basis for community benefit requirements in Scottish public contracts
⚖️
Procurement (Scotland) Regulations 2016
Sets out regulated procurement procedures and thresholds
🌿
Fair Work First Policy (2019 onwards)
Mandatory Fair Work conditions applied as a grant and contract condition
Contract Thresholds
Goods & Services£4,000,000+
Works Contracts£2,000,000+
Fair Work FirstAll public contracts
Fair Work First Requirements
Payment of at least the real Living Wage to all workers on the contract
No inappropriate use of zero-hours contracts
Genuine trade union recognition or effective workforce voice mechanisms
Investment in skills and training, including accredited provision
Action on gender pay gap and ethnicity pay gap

Wales
Capital: Cardiff · Senedd Cymru (Welsh Parliament)

Wales has the most ambitious sustainability-linked social value legislation in the UK. The Well-being of Future Generations Act 2015 creates a statutory duty across 7 well-being goals for all public bodies.

Key Legislation
🏴󠁧󠁢󠁷󠁬󠁳󠁿
Well-being of Future Generations (Wales) Act 2015
World-leading: statutory duty across 7 well-being goals for all public bodies
🤝
Social Partnership and Public Procurement (Wales) Act 2023
Socially responsible procurement as a statutory duty for Welsh public bodies
📄
Wales Procurement Policy Statement 2021
Community Benefits, ethical employment, low carbon and local economy
Contract Thresholds
Goods & Services£1,000,000+
Works Contracts£2,000,000+
Socially Responsible ProcurementAll public contracts
The 7 Well-being Goals
A prosperous Wales — innovative, productive, low carbon economy
A resilient Wales — maintaining and enhancing biodiversity and ecosystems
A healthier Wales — physical and mental well-being maximised
A more equal Wales — people fulfil their potential regardless of background
A Wales of cohesive communities — attractive, safe, viable places
A globally responsible Wales — a positive contribution to the world
Priority Areas
Well-being GoalsDecarbonisationCommunity BenefitsWelsh LanguageBiodiversity

Northern Ireland
Capital: Belfast · Northern Ireland Assembly (Stormont)

Northern Ireland operates under strong equality duties via Section 75 of the Northern Ireland Act 1998. Social clauses are actively encouraged through Central Procurement Directorate guidance.

Key Legislation & Policy
⚖️
Section 75, Northern Ireland Act 1998
Statutory equality and good relations duties on all public authorities
🏛️
Procurement Act 2023 (UK-wide)
Applies to reserved matters; social value principles embedded throughout
📋
CPD Social Clauses Guidance (2023)
Central Procurement Directorate guidance on embedding social clauses
Social Clause Framework
Works (Social Clauses Mandatory)£1,000,000+
Equality ScreeningAll public contracts
Good Relations DutyAll public authorities
Key Requirements
Social clauses in public works contracts encourage employment, training and apprenticeship commitments
Equality Impact Assessments required across 9 protected characteristics under Section 75
Good relations duty covering community relations, religious belief and political opinion
Priority targeting of long-term unemployed — NI has persistently higher inactivity rates than GB

National TOMs at a Glance
The National Themes, Outcomes and Measures (TOMs) framework — an open-source social value measurement standard created by the National Social Value Taskforce, covering 53 core and additional measures across 5 themes.
Theme 1 — Promote Local Skills and Employment
Jobs, Skills & Employment
Creating local employment opportunities, supporting disadvantaged groups into work, building skills through training and apprenticeships, and improving the employability of young people.
Theme 2 — Supporting Growth of Responsible Regional Business
Growth & Responsible Business
Growing the local supply chain, supporting VCSEs and MSMEs, improving staff wellbeing, promoting equality and fair pay, and embedding ethical procurement throughout the supply chain.
Theme 3 — Healthier, Safer and More Resilient Communities
Healthier, Safer Communities
Reducing crime and homelessness, creating healthier communities, supporting vulnerable people, and building stronger community connections through volunteering and local investment.
Theme 4 — Decarbonising and Safeguarding Our World
Environment & Decarbonisation
Reducing carbon emissions, improving air quality, safeguarding the natural environment, promoting circular economy solutions, and embedding sustainable procurement throughout the supply chain.
Theme 5 — Promoting Social Innovation
Social Innovation
Innovative measures that go beyond standard practice to create new approaches to local skills, responsible business, community resilience, and environmental protection.
